5    /**
6    * A radix trie or radix tree is a space-optimized trie data structure where
7    * each node with only one child is merged with its child. The result is that
8    * every internal node has at least two children. Unlike in regular tries, edges
9    * can be labeled with sequences of characters as well as single characters.
10    * This makes them much more efficient for small sets (especially if the strings
11    * are long) and for sets of strings that share long prefixes. This particular
12    * radix tree is used to represent an associative array.
13    * <p>
14    * This implementation is a composition of a PatriciaTrie as the backing
15    * structure.
16    * <p>
17    * @see <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radix_tree">Radix Tree / Patricia Trie (Wikipedia)</a>
